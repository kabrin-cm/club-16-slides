  • Active Instagram. 14K followers. ~1,700 posts. Consistent content output.

Current State

What needs to change

  • Ad traffic going to homepage. Too many options, too many clicks. Conversion will be low. Need dedicated landing pages per offer.
  • 3-day free pass is buried. It's on the site but not prominent. Should be the #1 offer for cold traffic ads.
  • Tracking infrastructure unverified. Meta Pixel + Conversions API must be confirmed. Without this, Meta can't optimize delivery.
  • No retargeting audiences built. Website visitors, video viewers, social engagers are being lost. This is the highest-ROI traffic.

  • No location-specific targeting. Each gym should have its own geo-targeted ad set within location clusters.
  • No post-signup nurture. Members sign up and then nothing. Need email/SMS sequence to drive first visit and retention.

Technical prerequisites before launch

Meta Pixel

Must be installed on trevorlindenfitness.com and firing correctly on all pages. Check via Events Manager > Test Events. If not installed, NOTHING works.

Ask your agency: "Is our pixel installed? Show me the Events Manager."

Conversions API (CAPI)

Server-side tracking that works alongside the pixel. Harder for ad blockers to block. Dramatically improves data quality and optimization.

Ask your agency: "Is CAPI configured? Are we getting server events?"

Campaign 1: Prospecting

Prospecting: The top of the funnel

Audience Setup

  • Broad local targeting: 5-15km per location cluster
  • Ages 18-65+ (don't restrict too much)
  • No interest stacking initially (let Andromeda work)
  • Exclude current member list
  • Exclude lead form submitters (last 30 days)
  • Exclude warm audiences (no overlap)

Why Broad Works

Andromeda is smarter than manual targeting. Give it diverse creative and let the algorithm find the buyers. Over-targeting limits reach and increases CPM.

Ad Set Structure

Option A: ABO by Cluster

5 ad sets, one per location cluster. $20/day each. You control budget per area. Best for testing phase.

Option B: CBO with Locations

One campaign, 5 ad sets, algorithm decides budget allocation. Best when you have data showing what works. Move to this in Month 2-3.

Creative Load

20-25 individual ads per ad set. Each genuinely different (Andromeda compliance). Mix of video, static, and carousel.

Campaign 1: Prospecting

Prospecting: Lead with the free pass

3-Day Free Pass
Primary offer for all cold traffic
"For cold traffic, the free pass is unbeatable. Zero cost. Zero commitment. Zero risk. The only thing they're giving you is an email and a phone number. The gym experience sells the membership."

Why not lead with $7.49/week?

Even at $7.49/week, cold traffic has higher friction with a paid offer. The free pass gets them through the door. Staff converts in person. Conversion rate on free trials is significantly higher than direct-to-paid.

Lead Form Setup

  • Question 1: Closest location (dropdown, all 17)
  • Question 2: Have you been a member before? (Y/N)
  • Question 3: Best phone number (pre-filled)
  • Max 3 questions. More = fewer completions.

The 5-Minute Rule

Text within 5 minutes of form submission. Call within 15. Fitness industry data shows 2.6x higher close rate when contacted in under 5 minutes vs 24+ hours. Over half of gym leads never get a response at all.

Campaign 2: Retargeting

Retargeting: The "Hammer Them" content strategy

Don't just run the same "Join Now" ad repeatedly. Create content across four pillars that addresses every reason someone hasn't signed up yet.

Questions

What do people need to know?

  • "What are your hours?"
  • "Do you have free weights?"
  • "What classes are included?"
  • "Is parking available?"
  • "What's the recovery centre?"

Objections

What's stopping them?

  • "I don't have time"
  • "I'm intimidated"
  • "It's too expensive"
  • "I won't stick with it"
  • "I don't know what to do"

Expectations

What will it be like?

  • "What happens on day 1?"
  • "How do I sign up for class?"
  • "What's the free orientation?"
  • "How long until I see results?"
  • "What should I bring?"

Social Proof

Who else is like me here?

  • Member testimonials (video)
  • Before/after transformations
  • Busy gym floor footage
  • Community event highlights
  • Trainer introductions

Create 3-5 pieces of content for each pillar. That's 12-20 retargeting ads that all feel different but serve one goal: removing doubt.

Ad Concept 1: "Price Shock" Reel

The Script

Hook (0-3s)

"I pay $7.49 a week for my gym. No contract. Let me show you what that actually gets me."

Body (3-25s)

[Walk through gym] "State-of-the-art cardio. Full free weight section. [Show machines] Every machine you'd find at a $60/month gym. [Show class] Unlimited group fitness classes. [Show recovery] Recovery centre. [Show locker room] Clean locker rooms."

CTA (25-35s)

Production Notes

  • Film with iPhone rear camera (wipe lens first)
  • Vertical 9:16 format (Reels/Stories)
  • Use Rode Wireless Go 2 lapel mic
  • Film during off-peak hours (cleaner shots)
  • Member or trainer on camera (not corporate)
  • Walk-and-talk format, keep moving
  • Natural lighting preferred
  • Add captions (80% watch without sound)
  • Add 4:5 text banner at top with hook line
  • 30-45 seconds total length

Why This Works

The gap between "$7.49/week" and what they SEE creates cognitive dissonance. They expect a budget gym. They see premium equipment, group classes, and recovery. That surprise is the conversion trigger.

Creative Strategy

Creative testing: The 50/30/20 model

50% Budget: Proven Winners

Your ads that are working. Highest ROAS. Keep running but create slight variations (different hook, same concept) to extend their life.

30% Budget: Adjacent Concepts

New ads similar to winners but exploring different angles. If "price shock" is winning, try "price shock at a different location" or "price shock from a member instead of trainer."

20% Budget: Experiments

Bold new concepts. A humorous take on gym intimidation. A day-in-the-life. A trainer spotlight. These are long shots that might become tomorrow's winners.

Traffic light decision framework

Check every Monday. Simple decision rules for every campaign and ad set.

GREEN

CPL under $20. Leads converting to trials.

  • Keep running. Consider scaling.
  • Increase budget 20% every 3 days.
  • Duplicate to new location clusters.
  • Create adjacent variations of winning ads.

YELLOW

CPL $20-35. Some leads, inconsistent.

  • Watch closely this week. Don't scale.
  • Don't panic. Don't make changes yet.
  • Check: is it the hook? The audience? The offer?
  • If Yellow for 7+ days, test new creative.

RED

CPL above $35. Low CTR. Few leads.

  • Pause the ad set (not the campaign).
  • Diagnose: high CPM = creative issue.
  • Low CTR = hook isn't working.
  • Refresh creative. Test new hooks. Relaunch.

Pro Tip: Budget Weight by Density

Downtown Vancouver and Brentwood should get more budget per location than Chilliwack. More people in radius = more potential leads per dollar. Weight budget proportionally to population density within each cluster.

Lower-Ticket Playbook

At $7.49/week, value positioning is everything

Lead with Value, Not Just Price

At $7.49/week (~$32/month), you're competitively priced but not the cheapest in the market. The winning angle: same or better equipment as $60+/month gyms, no contract, and Trevor Linden's name behind it. Price is a supporting detail. Value is the headline.

Speed to Conversion

Short funnels. Maximum 3 form fields. Mobile-first everything. Free pass signup should take under 30 seconds. Every extra step loses 20-30% of people.

Impulse-Friendly Framing

"About a dollar a day." "$7.49/week. No contract." "Premium gym. Budget price." The no-contract angle is your biggest differentiator. Competitors lock people in. You don't.

Social Proof at Scale

"17 locations. Thousands of members. No contract." At a competitive price point, trust signals matter more. Member count, location count, Trevor Linden association, years in business.

The Retention Truth

Ads get them in the door. The experience keeps them paying. A member who stays 3 years at $7.49/week = $1,170+ LTV. A member who quits after 1 month = $32 LTV. The post-signup experience is where the profit lives.
