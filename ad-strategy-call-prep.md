# Club 16 Meta Ad Strategy
## Call Prep - March 5, 2026

---

## THE OPPORTUNITY

Club 16 has a strong competitive position in the BC fitness market. At $7.49/week (~$32/month) with no contract, you're priced below most competitors while offering premium equipment, group classes, recovery, and the Trevor Linden brand. The ad strategy: lead with value, get people through the door with zero-commitment trials, and let the experience sell the membership.

The $10/month promo expires March 31. That's 26 days of urgency we can use right now. At $10/month vs regular ~$32/month, that promo is a massive discount.

---

## 1. CURRENT STATE: WHAT'S WORKING AND WHAT'S NOT

### What's Working
- **Price is highly competitive.** $7.49/week (~$32/month). Cheaper than most competitors, with better value and no contract.
- **Trevor Linden brand.** Instant credibility with BC locals. Sports hero trust transfer.
- **17 locations.** Covers the entire Lower Mainland. Convenience is a real selling point.
- **She's FIT sub-brand.** 6 women-only locations. No competitor has this. Untapped ad angle.
- **No contracts.** Removes the #1 objection to gym signups. This should be in EVERY ad.
- **Instagram presence.** 14K followers, ~1,700 posts. Consistent output.
- **3-day free pass.** Exists on the website. Good lead magnet.

### What Needs to Change
- **Ads should NOT go to the homepage.** Too many options, too many clicks. Need dedicated landing pages per offer.
- **3-day free pass is buried.** Should be the primary ad offer for cold traffic. Front and center.
- **Need tracking infrastructure.** Meta Pixel + Conversions API must be installed and firing correctly. Without this, Meta can't optimize. This is job #1.
- **No retargeting running.** Website visitors, video viewers, and social engagers should all be in retargeting audiences. This is free money being left on the table.
- **Creative variety is low.** Need UGC-style content (member testimonials, facility walkthroughs, value reaction videos). Polished corporate content underperforms on Meta right now.
- **No location-specific targeting.** Each gym should have its own geo-targeted ad set.

## 2. CAMPAIGN ARCHITECTURE

Three campaigns. Simple structure. Let Meta's AI do the heavy lifting.

### Campaign 1: PROSPECTING (60% of budget)
**Goal:** New leads who've never heard of Club 16
**Audience:** Broad local targeting, 5-15km radius around each location, ages 18-65+
**Offer:** 3-day free guest pass (zero commitment, zero risk)
**Creative:** UGC member testimonials, facility tour Reels, value shock content
**Optimization:** Leads (lead form or landing page conversion)

### Campaign 2: RETARGETING (25% of budget)
**Goal:** Convert people who already know about Club 16
**Audience:** Website visitors (180 days), video viewers (365 days), IG/FB engagers
**Offer:** $10/month promo with March 31 deadline
**Creative:** Urgency-driven ($10/month countdown), testimonials, "still thinking about it?" messaging
**Optimization:** Conversions (signup completion)

### Campaign 3: GEO-CONQUEST (15% of budget)
**Goal:** Steal members from competitors
**Audience:** People near Anytime Fitness, Fitness World, and GoodLife locations in BC
**Offer:** "$7.49/week vs what you're paying now" comparison
**Creative:** Side-by-side price comparison, "switch and save" messaging
**Optimization:** Leads

## 5. FUNNEL STRUCTURE

### The Problem Right Now
If ad traffic goes to the homepage, conversion will be low. Too many options. Too many clicks. People bounce.

### What to Build

**Funnel A: Free Trial Funnel (Cold Traffic)**
```
Meta Ad (3-day free pass)
  -> Dedicated landing page: "Your 3-Day Free Pass"
    -> Lead form: name, email, phone, preferred location
      -> Thank you page + email/SMS confirmation
        -> Reminder sequence (day before visit)
          -> Staff converts in-person
```

**Funnel B: Promo Funnel (Retargeting)**
```
Meta Ad ($10/month, expires 3/31)
  -> Promo landing page with countdown timer
    -> Online signup (select location, payment)
      -> Confirmation + first visit guide email
```

**Funnel C: She's FIT Funnel (Women-Specific)**
```
Meta Ad (women 25-55)
  -> She's FIT landing page (women-only messaging)
    -> Free pass or direct signup
      -> Welcome email with class schedule
```

**Key principle:** One offer per page. One CTA per page. Mobile-first design.

## 10. VALUE POSITIONING STRATEGY

At $7.49/week (~$32/month), the positioning is "best value" not "cheapest." The strategy adapts accordingly:

**Lead with value, not just price.** You're competitively priced, not the cheapest. The winning angle: same or better equipment as $60+/month gyms, no contract, and Trevor Linden's name behind it.

**No contract is the key differentiator.** Competitors lock people in. You don't. This removes the biggest objection and should be in every ad.

**Speed to conversion.** Short funnels. Minimal form fields. Mobile-first. Free pass signup should take under 30 seconds.

**Impulse-friendly framing.** Reframe the price:
- "About a dollar a day"
- "$7.49/week. No contract."
- "Premium gym. Budget price."

**Retention is the real game.** Ads get them in the door. The experience keeps them paying. Recommend a post-signup email nurture sequence:
- Day 1: Welcome + first visit guide
- Day 3: "How was your first visit?"
- Day 7: Group class recommendation
- Day 14: Personal training intro offer (upsell)
- Day 30: "You've been a member for a month!" celebration

Member staying 12 months at $7.49/week = $390 LTV. Acquisition cost of $75 = 5.2x return. A member who stays 3 years = $1,170+ LTV.
